# Environment Assets

Book VI — Assets

## Overview

Environment assets define the virtual world in which users interact. They include terrain, vegetation, buildings, props, sky systems, and environmental effects that establish the setting, scale, and atmosphere of a scene.

## Asset Categories

- Terrain and Landscapes
- Buildings and Architecture
- Vegetation
- Props and Decorations
- Roads and Pathways
- Water Bodies
- Skyboxes and HDRI Environments

## Design Considerations

- Modular asset creation
- Level of Detail (LOD)
- Consistent scale and units
- Reusable asset libraries
- Performance-aware scene composition

## Optimization

- Occlusion culling
- Instancing
- Texture atlasing
- Asset streaming
- Baked lighting where appropriate

Well-structured environment assets create immersive worlds while maintaining rendering efficiency and scalability.
