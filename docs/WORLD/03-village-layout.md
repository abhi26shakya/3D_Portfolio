# Village Layout

# BOOK IV — WORLD

## Purpose
Define the physical arrangement of the Japanese-inspired village so that architecture, landscape and portfolio content form one coherent journey.

## VILLAGE-001 Layout Philosophy
The village should feel authentic, walkable and peaceful. The road is the primary guide, with secondary paths leading to optional interactive details.

## VILLAGE-002 Zones
1. Entrance Gate
2. Residential Area (Childhood Home)
3. Village School & Library
4. Riverside Market
5. Electronics Workshop
6. Rice Fields & Drone Practice Area
7. Forest Path
8. Research Campus
9. Collaboration Square
10. Cherry Blossom Avenue
11. Red Phone Booth
12. Mountain Shrine

## VILLAGE-003 Natural Elements
- Flowing river connecting multiple zones.
- Wooden bridges.
- Bamboo groves.
- Cherry blossom trees.
- Stone lanterns.
- Mountain backdrop visible throughout the journey.

## VILLAGE-004 Navigation
The main road must remain visible at all times. Secondary paths reveal optional interactions but always reconnect to the main route.

## VILLAGE-005 Environmental Storytelling
Each zone should contain visual clues about the associated portfolio section before text appears, allowing visitors to anticipate the next chapter naturally.

## Acceptance Criteria
The village should feel like a believable place that exists independently of the portfolio while seamlessly guiding visitors through Abhishek's story.
