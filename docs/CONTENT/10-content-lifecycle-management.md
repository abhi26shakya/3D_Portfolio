# Content Lifecycle Management

Book VII — Content

## Overview

Content lifecycle management (CLM) is the structured process of planning, creating, reviewing, publishing, maintaining, archiving, and retiring content. A well-defined lifecycle ensures information remains accurate, relevant, and valuable throughout the project's evolution.

## Lifecycle Stages

1. Planning
2. Creation
3. Review
4. Approval
5. Publication
6. Maintenance
7. Archival
8. Retirement

## Roles

- Content Authors
- Reviewers
- Editors
- Maintainers
- Stakeholders

## Best Practices

- Assign ownership for every content item.
- Schedule periodic reviews.
- Archive obsolete content instead of deleting it immediately.
- Track revisions through version control.
- Measure content effectiveness and continuously improve.

A mature content lifecycle management process keeps documentation and media trustworthy, discoverable, and sustainable over the long term.
