# Story Bible

# BOOK II — STORY

## Purpose
The portfolio tells a single continuous story rather than presenting disconnected webpages. The visitor follows Abhishek's journey from childhood curiosity to engineering, research, and future ambitions.

## Story Statement
The road represents life. Every step forward reveals a new chapter. The visitor is not controlling a game character—they are accompanying Abhishek throughout his journey.

## Narrative Structure
1. Prologue — Dawn in a peaceful Japanese-inspired village.
2. Childhood — Curiosity, family, dreams.
3. Education — School, learning, discipline.
4. Engineering — Electronics, robotics, programming.
5. Research — AI, neuroscience, publications, innovation.
6. Experience — Internships, collaboration, open source.
7. Future — The road continues toward the mountains.
8. Epilogue — 'The Journey Continues.'

## Story Principles
- Show, don't tell.
- The environment introduces each chapter before UI text appears.
- Every interaction should reinforce personal growth.
- The portfolio remains the focus; the world exists to support it.

## Symbols
- Road = Life journey
- Bridge = Transition
- River = Continuous learning
- Lantern = Knowledge
- Mountain = Long-term goals
- Telephone Booth = Invitation to connect

## Acceptance Criteria
- Visitors understand the narrative without needing explicit explanations.
- Each chapter transitions naturally into the next.
- The story remains professional and portfolio-focused, never becoming a game.
