# Story Flow

## Objective
Define the complete visitor journey from arrival to departure. Every transition should feel like a continuous walk through one living world rather than navigation between webpages.

## Chapter 1 — The First Step
A quiet Japanese-inspired village at sunrise. A young boy stands on a country road. After a short pause, he begins walking. The visitor gains scroll control.

## Chapter 2 — Curiosity
The road passes homes, fields and a village school. The About section is introduced naturally.

## Chapter 3 — Learning
The environment evolves into classrooms, books, electronics benches and laboratories. Education and technical foundations are presented.

## Chapter 4 — Engineering
The character becomes a teenager. Robotics, drones, circuit boards and embedded systems appear. Projects become interactive exhibits.

## Chapter 5 — Research
The character grows into a young researcher. The environment transitions toward a modern research district while preserving elements of the original village. Research, publications and internships are revealed.

## Chapter 6 — Collaboration
The world feels larger and more connected. Experience, teamwork and leadership are highlighted through interactions with mentors and colleagues.

## Chapter 7 — Contact
At dusk, the character reaches a glowing telephone booth. Lifting the receiver reveals contact details, resume download, GitHub and LinkedIn.

## Epilogue
The camera rises to reveal the complete journey. The road continues toward distant mountains with the message: 'The Journey Continues.'

## Transition Rules
- Never use hard scene cuts.
- Use lighting, terrain and architecture to indicate progression.
- The visitor should always feel they are walking forward through one continuous world.
